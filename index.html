Conservation Status & Species Profiles
Species A · Freshwater
Aral Trout Salmo trutta aralensis
Regionally Extinct (RE)
Salinity Tolerance Very low (<8 g/L). As a salmonid, highly sensitive to osmotic stress. Cannot regulate ion balance in brackish water.
Aral Sea Fate Extirpated from the Aral Sea by the 1980s. No confirmed records for >50 years. Likely globally extinct.
Primary Threat Salinity increase, river diversion, dam construction blocking spawning migration routes.
IUCN Source iucnredlist.org ↗
Species B · Moderate Tolerance
Aral Barbel Luciobarbus brachycephalus brachycephalus
Endangered (EN)
Salinity Tolerance Moderate (up to ~18–20 g/L). Adapted to the original brackish Aral (baseline ~10 g/L) but physiologically stressed beyond this.
Aral Sea Fate Extirpated from the sea. Small populations persist in middle/upper Amu Darya and Zeravshan rivers only.
Primary Threat Salinity increase, dam construction, habitat fragmentation, overfishing of remaining riverine populations.
IUCN Source iucnredlist.org ↗
Species C · Highly Euryhaline
Ninespine Stickleback Pungitius platygaster aralensis
Least Concern (LC)
Salinity Tolerance High (tolerates 28–35 g/L). The only native fish to persist in the Aral Sea throughout the regression crisis.
Aral Sea Fate Survived the salinity increase that killed all other native species. Has recolonised the North (Small) Aral Sea following partial restoration.
Key Significance Living evidence of natural selection under abiotic stress. Its survival is directly comparable to the simulation's Species C curve.
IUCN Source iucnredlist.org ↗
