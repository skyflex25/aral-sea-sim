Conservation Status & Species Profiles
Species A · Freshwater
Aral Trout Salmo trutta aralensis
Regionally Extinct (RE)
Salinity Tolerance Very low (<8 g/L). Highly sensitive to osmotic stress as a salmonid; cannot regulate ion balance in brackish water.
Aral Sea Fate Extirpated by the 1980s. No confirmed records for >50 years. Likely globally extinct.
Primary Threats Salinity increase, river diversion, dam construction blocking spawning migrations.
IUCN Source iucnredlist.org ↗
Species B · Moderate Tolerance
Aral Barbel Luciobarbus brachycephalus brachycephalus
Endangered (EN)
Salinity Tolerance Moderate (up to ~18–20 g/L). Adapted to original brackish Aral (~10 g/L baseline) but physiologically stressed beyond this.
Aral Sea Fate Extirpated from the sea. Small populations persist in the middle/upper Amu Darya and Zeravshan rivers.
Primary Threats Salinity increase, dam construction, habitat fragmentation, overfishing.
IUCN Source iucnredlist.org ↗
Species C · Highly Euryhaline
Ninespine Stickleback Pungitius platygaster aralensis
Least Concern (LC)
Salinity Tolerance High (tolerates 28–35 g/L). The only native fish to persist throughout the Aral Sea regression crisis.
Aral Sea Fate Survived total replacement of the native fish community. Recolonised the North Aral following partial restoration via the Kokaral Dam.
Key Significance Living evidence of natural selection under abiotic stress — directly mirrors the Species C curve in this simulation.
IUCN Source iucnredlist.org ↗
